SUBJECT: Artho-M-026
ORIGIN: Lunar outpost "Selena-4", free protogen sector.
CHASSIS: Experimental "Light-Frame" model (no external armor).

HISTORY:
Artho didn't plan to visit Earth. On his home moon, he worked as a systems technician in the spatial shift laboratory. The M-026 model was intentionally created without heavy armor plates: its chassis was designed for maximum conductivity to work with subtle energies and quantum fields. This made him physically vulnerable, but incredibly sensitive to any systemic fluctuations.

Everything changed during the testing of the experimental teleportation node "Aether-1". At the moment of launch, a critical resonance occurred with an external radio noise source originating from the Solar System. As a result of the quantum collapse, Artho was literally "plucked" out of the laboratory.

His molecular structure was reassembled at random coordinates — on the outskirts of Nizhny Novgorod. Due to the lack of armor and protection from overloads, Artho's navigation systems were damaged, and communication with his home moon was completely severed. Now, having adapted to local conditions and using his nanites to maintain system stability, he studies this strange world, trying to understand: was this jump an error, or did someone (or something) on Earth intentionally pull him in.

System Note: The M-026 model does not use standard servo drives in its joints. Instead, phased magnetic clutches are installed.

Log entry from "Selena-4": > "Subject Artho demonstrates abnormally high neural interface bandwidth. In the 'Aether-1' lab environment, he was the only one who 'heard' the quantum field noise before the sensors detected it."
